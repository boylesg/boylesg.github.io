Coal fired versus oil/diesel fired

A coal fired steam train all the way from Southern Cross is likely to be impractical in terms of coal re-fueling instrastructure along the way. So for practical and efficiency reasons any steam train doing this loop would need to be diesel or oil fired.

The trains could have the appearance of 19th century and/or early 20th century coal fired locomotives, with a fake coal bunker to complete the illusions, but be oil fired 'under the hood'. The existing restored coal fired steam locomotives, operating out of Maldon and Ballarat etc., could contribute to the fun for toutists and locals, with short runs through sections of the loop.

Steam locomotive construction

Why not build the steam locomotives, doing the long haul from Southern Cross station from scratch, to fit the modern gauge, in the goldfields towns. There could potentially be opportunities for a niche export market, either to other tourist hot spots in Australia. Or even overseas.

This would spawn a whole range of construction and engineering jobs for the goldfield's school leavers, residents and partial retirees alike. And keep alive dying trades.

Regular VLine service

The steam train service ought be coordinated with regular VLine services so that tourists can return to Southern Cross the same day or the next day if they don't wish to stay in the goldfields for more than a day or overnight. There would also be scope for Vline services to traverse the loop and provide a more frequent service to and from Maryborough.
