Small town feel

No one wants to turn Maryborough into a major regional center like Ballarat or Bendigo. So Maryborough will need to share the tourist load with the other towns along the rail line and nearby. And the frequency of the steam service will need to be carefully considered to get the right balance between maintaining the small town feel with a steady flow of tourists and income for the town and council generated from them. It will not be set and forget proposition, rather continuously monitored, with regular community consultation.

Perhaps a once per week or once per fortnight steam service, combined with regular VLine services, could be trialed for a period of time. Not entirely meeting total tourist demand from Southern Cross may be a good thing as it will create a sense of exclusivity for those who do get a seat on the steam train. Economics 101 tells us that high demand with low supply means higher prices for Maryborough and other towns.

Local youth

Important for the endeavour to succeed will be getting local youth on board and give them back a sense that there is hope for a good and propsorous life in Maryborough. Some of them may be interested particularly in leading or assisting with bicycle tours of the digging and ruins for example. Or leading small parties of tourists in the rail trail between Maryborough and Castlemaine. This would be an oppotunity for them to learn valuable customer service and human relations skills.
