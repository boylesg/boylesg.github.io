Trees

Acacia pycnantha / Golden Wattle

The origin of Australia's Olympic green & gold.

Banksia marginata / Silver Banksia

These are small to medium trees.

The flowers are rich in nectar and attract a wide variety of native birds.

Banksia praemorsa / Cut Leaf Banksia

These are small to medium trees.

The flowers are rich in nectar and attract a wide variety of native birds.

There are varieties with both lemon yellow and burgundy colored flowers.

Brachychiton_acerifolius / Flame Tree

In Victoria these grow as small to medium trees.

They are deciduous over summer.

They put on a spectacular display from spring to summer.

Brachychiiton rupestris / Bottle Tree

This is a small tree in Victoria.

The trunk swells hugely when the ground is wet and contracts when it is dry, so this is not a regular woody tree.

It is in fact Australia's version of a succulent plant.

It is a striking feature tree for small gardens.

Corymbia ficifolia / Flowering Gum

In Victoria these grow as small to medium trees.

Eucalyptus pauciflora / Snow Gum

Outside the alpine areas these grow as small to medium trees.

Hymenosporum_flavum / Native Frangipanni

In Victoria these grow as small trees.

The flowers have a similar fragrance to exotic fragipanis.

Macadamia integrifolia / Macadamia Nut

In Victoria these grow as small to medium trees, and they will produce those delicious nuts with 2-3 years of planting.

They need a little TLC to get them established in our cooler climate however, once established, they are quite drought tolerant.

Large Shrubs

Backhousia citriodora / Lemon Myrtle

The foliage has a strong lemon fragranace and flavour. It can be used as a lemon flavouring in cooking and drinks, as well as potpourri.

Banksia menziesii / Firewood Banksia

Unusual serrated foliage and spectacular candle flowers.

Callistemon 'Kings Park Special'

Massed of scarlet bottle brush flowers from spring to summer.

Myoporum viscosum / Sticky Boobialla

Great fast growing screening shrub covered with white flowers through spring.

Nothofagus cunninghamii / Southern Beech

Great screen shrub for sheltered areas and moist soils.

Prostanthera lasianthos / Christmas Mint Bush

Great screening shrub for shletered areas and moist soils. Their foliage has a strong minty fragrance and are covered in white flowers in December.

Prostanthera ovalifolia / Oval Leaf Mint Bush

Great screening shrub for drier soils. Their foliage has a strong minty fragrance and are covered in white flowers during spring.

Rapnaea howittiana / Dogwood

Great screen shrub with tough but lush looking foliage.

Tasmannia lanceolata / Mountain Pepper

A very attractive shrub with bright red stems and dark green foiage. Both the berries and foliage have a strong peppery taste and can be used in place of traditional peppers.

Telopea speciosissima / NSW Waratah

Spectacular scarlet flowers in spring an a great screen shrub.

Boronia heterophylla / Lipstick Boronia

Corea reflexa / Native Fucia

Excellent screen shrub and very drought tolerant.

Darwinia meeboldii / Cranbrook Bell

Spectacular flowers and a terrific screening shrub.

Indigofera australis / Austral Indigo

Very drought tolerant and great screening shrub.

Isopogon latifolius

Spectacular feature shrub.

Olearia phlogopappa / Alpine Daisy Bush

Spectacular screening shrub.

Pimelia nivea / Round leaf Rice Flower

Spectacular screening shrub.

Prostanthera rotundifolia / Native Oregano
