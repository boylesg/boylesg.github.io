Colors specified via red, green and blue amounts

Red, green and blue are primary colors, that is they are not made by combining other colors. On the other hand yelow is made by combining red and green, cyan is made by combining blue and green, magenta is made by combining red and blue and white is made by combining red, green and blue. Cyan, yellow, magenta and white are referred to as secondary colors.

Now by varying the amount of red, green and blue you can create any color that you can imagine. This is where rgb colors come in. In CSS an rgb color is specified by a call to the Javascript function 'rgb(amount_red, amount_green, amount_blue)'. The parameters amount_red, amount_green, amount_blue specify the amount of red, green and blue light and should be numbers between 0 and 255, e.g. rgb(50, 250, 100). What color is that? Here it is!

You can also use the JavaScript function 'rgb(amount_red, amount_green, amount_blue, transparency)'. The first 3 parameters are the same as for the function 'rgb(...)'. The fourth parameter specifies the tasparency of the color and your be a number between 0 and 1 where 0 is fully transparent and 1 is fully opaque, e.g. rgb(50, 250, 100, 0.5). Colors specified via hue, saturation and lightness

HSL colors are specified via a call to this JavaScript function: hsl(hue, saturation, lightness). The parametsr of this function are as follows:

  • hue: this must be between 0 an 360 and represents an angle on the color wheel. So 0 dgree would give you red, 150 degrees would give you green and 300 degrees would give you blue.


  • saturation: this must be between 0 and 100 and represents how greyed the color appears. 0 (0%) means the color just appears as grey while 100 (100%) means there is no greying of the color at all.


  • lightness: this must be between 0 and 100 and represents how darkened or lightened the color appears. 0 (0%) means the color appears as black, 100 (100%) means that the color appears as pure white and 50 (50%) means that the color is normal.

Hex colors

Red, green and blue are primary colors, that is they are not made by combining other colors. On the other hand yelow is made by combining red and green, cyan is made by combining blue and green, magenta is made by combining red and blue and white is made by combining red, green and blue. Cyan, yellow, magenta and white are referred to as secondary colors.

Now by varying the amount of red, green and blue you can create any color that you can imagine. This is where hex colors come in. In CSS a hex color is flagged via the character #. Following that there should be 3 sets of 2 digit hex integers that specify the amount of red, green and blue light:

# 03 A6 D5

Those spaces are there just to make things clearer for you and you would not normally include them. But 03 is the amount of red light, A6 is the amount of green light and D5 is the amount of blue light. What color is that? Here it is!
