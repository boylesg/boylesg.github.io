My pedogogy with mathematics, chemistry, physics and coding is old fashioned one. It involves lots of repition and practice, with a given problem type, until the steps in solving them become almost a reflex action for the student. Once that is acheived it leaves 'spare' mental capacity that often allows the student to develop a deeper understanding of the underlying concepts. In much the same way that an apprentice carpenter must become automatically profficient in the use a drill, hammer and chisel before they are ready to construct a beautiful cabinet.

I don't subscribe to the currently fashionable theory of learning styles i.e. visual, auditory, reading & writing and kinesthetic. I do not believe it is a question of one or another of these for any particular student, but rather all of the above for every student. The dependancies on each of these largely varies with what is being taught, rather than with the student. And there is plenty of evidence that suggests that fundamentally different learning styles is just pseudoscience.

I believe that learning by doing is by far the best way for most people to learn. Lecture or chalk board style learning is both boring to deliver and boring to receive. And few people, inlcuding me, retain a signficant amount of material presented. It is only when you start applying the concepts that the pieces really start to fall into place within one's grey matter.

So pariticularly with coding and digital electronics, I prefer to get students away from lecture notes and tutorials and onto personal projects. The necessity to learn in order to complete a specific personal project can be a very powerful inentive to learn quickly. And working on a personal project over an extended period of time is just so much more fun than listening to teacher drone on in front of a chalkboard or whiteboard.

And, with personal projects, I am not shy in pushing students some what out of their comfort zones. At Altona North Coder Dojo we are often quite surprised as to what even some primary school students are capable of learning, that many adults would struggle to learn.
